Volopay Growth Squad Assignment
Context
Volopay’s Growth Squad works across content, community, sales, partnerships, operations,
AI workflows, and internal systems.
This role is for people who can think clearly, work with ambiguity, move fast, use tools
smartly, and create practical output without needing step-by-step instructions.
This assignment has three tasks. We are looking for ownership, clarity, execution quality,
attention to detail, and practical problem-solving.

Task 1: Finance Content Challenge
Create a LinkedIn content series for the Finance audience.
Write 3 LinkedIn posts for finance professionals such as CFOs, finance managers, finance
operators, controllers, AP/AR teams, and FP&A professionals.
The posts should make finance work feel practical, relatable, and useful.
The goal is not to promote Volopay directly.
The goal is to create content that finance teams would find relevant enough to read, relate
to, and respond to.
What to submit
1. Three LinkedIn posts.
2. A short note on who each post is written for.
3. A short note on why the topic would matter to that audience.
We will evaluate this based on relevance, originality, writing quality, clarity, and whether the
posts feel genuinely relatable to finance teams.

Task 2: AI Tool-Building Challenge
Complete a technical project using Claude, any AI model, automation tool, no-code platform,
API, or programming language. The implementation is entirely up to you. The tool does not
need to be perfect or complex. It should solve the problem clearly.

Your task is to build a simple tool that brings together information from different business
data sources and turns it into something useful for a sales, growth, or customer-facing team.
Problem statement
In a growing company, customer information is often scattered across multiple systems.
For example:
● CRM data from Zoho
● Support ticket intent or issue data
● Customer emails
● Notes from internal teams
● Conversation on slack
● Follow-up history
● Product usage or account status data
This makes it difficult for a team member to quickly understand:
● What is happening with a customer or lead
● Whether there are any risks or open issues
● What the customer cares about
● What the next best action should be
Your task is to build a tool that aggregates information from multiple sources and presents a
clear, useful view.
You do not need access to real Zoho, ticketing, or email data. You can create realistic
dummy data.
What your tool should do
Your tool should be able to:
1. Take in data from at least 3 different sources.
2. Combine the information into one useful view.
3. Summarise the key account/customer context.
4. Highlight risks, opportunities, or important signals.
5. Recommend the next best action.
6. Show the output in a simple and understandable format.
What to submit
1. A short explanation of the problem you solved.
2. The tool, prototype, workflow, or demo.
3. Screenshots, links, or a short screen recording showing how it works.
4. The dummy data you used.
5. The prompts, logic, workflow, or tools used to build it.
6. Examples input and output.

7. One short note on how you would improve it with more time.
We will evaluate this based on problem-solving ability, practical execution, smart use of
Claude/AI, clarity of output, and whether the tool feels useful in a real business context.

Task 3: Spreadsheet Dashboard Challenge
A growing sales team receives leads from multiple channels every day.
As volume increases, information becomes fragmented. Follow-ups are missed, duplicate
records appear, owners lose track of priorities, and it becomes difficult to understand where
opportunities are getting stuck.
Your task is to create a spreadsheet dashboard that helps a sales or growth team manage
its lead pipeline.
You may use Google Sheets or Excel.
The dashboard should help answer
1. How many leads came in.
2. Where the leads came from.
3. Which leads are still open.
4. Which leads need follow-up.
5. Which channels are performing best.
6. Where leads are getting stuck.
7. Which opportunities are high priority.
8. What actions the team should take next.
What to submit
1. A spreadsheet dashboard.
2. A sample data sheet with realistic dummy data.
3. Clear columns, formulas, filters, and views.
4. Charts or summaries where useful.
5. A short explanation of how a sales/growth manager should use the dashboard.
6. Three insights from your own sample data.
7. Three improvements you would make if this were used by a real team.
We will evaluate this based on structure, clarity, usefulness, attention to detail, data thinking,
and whether the dashboard would actually help a team operate better.

Submission Guidelines
Recommended format:
● Task 1: Google Doc or PDF
● Task 2: Link, screenshots, Loom/video, document, or working prototype
● Task 3: Google Sheet
Please keep the submission practical and easy to review.
We will evaluate your assignment based on:
1. Clarity of thinking
2. Ownership
3. Practicality
4. Execution quality
5. Attention to detail
6. Ability to work with ambiguity
7. Smart use of AI/tools
8. Communication clarity
There is no single correct answer. We care about how you think, how you execute, and
whether your output feels usable in the real world.
